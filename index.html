Best Time to Visit Vietnam

  • North Vietnam: March–May & September–November (cool, dry, best for trekking and sightseeing)
  • Central Vietnam: February–August (beaches and cities are sunny, but watch for typhoons in late summer)
  • South Vietnam: October–March (dry, sunny, best for islands and Ho Chi Minh City)

Top Festivals in Vietnam

  • Tet (Lunar New Year): Vietnam’s biggest celebration, usually in late January or February. Streets come alive with flowers, food, and fireworks.
  • Mid-Autumn Festival: Lantern parades and mooncakes in September or October, especially magical for families and children.
  • Hue Festival: Biennial cultural festival in Hue with music, art, and traditional performances.
  • Hoi An Lantern Festival: Monthly, on the full moon, the old town glows with thousands of lanterns.
  • Da Nang International Fireworks Festival: World-class fireworks competition held every summer, lighting up the Han River.
  • Phu Quoc New Year’s Festivals: Beach parties, fireworks, and live music on the island’s main beaches every December 31st.

Best Local Dishes in Vietnam

  • Banh Mi: Vietnamese baguette sandwich with pork, pate, pickled veggies, and herbs
  • Pho: Fragrant beef or chicken noodle soup with fresh herbs
  • Bun Cha: Grilled pork with rice noodles and fresh greens
  • Goi Cuon: Fresh spring rolls with shrimp, pork, and herbs
  • Com Tam: Broken rice with grilled pork chop and egg
  • Cao Lau: Savory noodles with pork, greens, and crispy croutons (Hoi An specialty)
  • Seafood BBQ: Grilled seafood, especially in coastal cities like Nha Trang

Western Restaurants in Vietnam

  • Pizza 4P's: Pizza 4P's (HCMC, Hanoi, Danang, Nha Trang) – Japanese-Italian fusion, famous for cheese made in-house.
  • El Gaucho Steakhouse: El Gaucho (HCMC, Hanoi, Danang) – Argentinian steakhouse, top-rated for Western dining.
  • La Villa French Restaurant: La Villa (HCMC) – Fine French cuisine in a colonial villa.
  • Skewers Restaurant: Skewers (HCMC) – Mediterranean grill, popular with expats.
  • Louisiane Brewhouse: Louisiane Brewhouse (Nha Trang) – Craft beer and Western food on the beach.

Visa & Travel Info

Official E-visa site:
Vietnam Government E-Visa Portal

  • North Vietnam: Most travelers arrive via Hanoi (Noi Bai Airport). Visa on arrival is available with e-visa. Note: Border crossings from China may have different rules.
  • Central Vietnam: Da Nang & Hue accept e-visas at airports and land borders. Ideal for exploring central coast and mountains.
  • South Vietnam: Ho Chi Minh City is the main gateway. E-visa accepted at Tan Son Nhat airport and main land crossings from Cambodia.
